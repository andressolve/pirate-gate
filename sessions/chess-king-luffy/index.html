The Session — ~45 minutes
04
Design
Describe the King
10 min
›

Before anything gets generated, describe your Luffy King chess piece to Claude Code. A chess piece that IS Luffy — not Luffy holding a chess piece. Claude will ask you questions to sharpen the vision.

💬 Say this to Claude Code
I want to design a King chess piece featuring Monkey D. Luffy. Let me describe what I see.
Then describe your vision. Claude will ask follow-up questions to get the details right.
  • ♔ Chess piece shape. Classic round chess base or something custom? How does the silhouette read as a King piece?
  • 🎩 The straw hat = the crown. On his head? Tilted? Around his neck? This is what makes the King a King.
  • 💪 Luffy's pose. Standing tall? Arms crossed? Fist up? Arms close to body print better — stretched-out Gum Gum poses can snap off.
  • 🎨 Which Luffy? Pre-timeskip (red vest, blue shorts)? Post-timeskip (open shirt, chest scar)? Gear Fifth? Your own version?
  • 📏 The base. Smooth cylinder? Ocean waves? Skull and crossbones? The Going Merry's deck?
Claude Code Your imagination
05
Create
See the King
15 min
›

Claude generates concept art based on your description. You'll see your Luffy King for the first time. Then you iterate — the first image is never the final image.

💬 Say this to Claude Code
Generate concept art of my Luffy King chess piece. I want to see the full piece — hat, pose, base, everything.

When the image appears, look carefully. What's right? What needs to change? Tell Claude: "The hat is right but the base needs to be taller" or "Make him look more confident." Usually takes 2–4 rounds.

🎨
Two versions. Claude generates a dramatic version (for you to see) and a clean version (optimized for the 3D conversion software). Same design, different format. The clean one is what becomes the 3D model.
  1. Tell Claude to generate concept art.
  2. Review. Say what's right and what to change.
  3. Iterate until you see YOUR Luffy King.
  4. Claude generates the clean version for 3D. Lock it in.
Claude Code Gemini (image gen)
06
Build
Into the Third Dimension
10 min
›

Claude sends your concept art to Meshy and builds the 3D model. Same Meshy you've used before — but called directly from the conversation. No switching apps.

💬 Say this to Claude Code
Turn my concept art into a 3D model. Use image-to-3D.
Takes about a minute. Claude opens a 3D viewer in your browser so you can rotate the model and check every angle.

Rotate the model. Check the straw hat, the pose, the base. Does it capture the energy of your design? If not, tell Claude what's off and try again.

💡
Image-to-3D, always. Starting from your concept art produces way better results than describing the model in text. That's why we drew it first.
Claude Code Meshy MCP Image to 3D 3D Viewer
07
Forge
Ready for the Printer
10 min
›

The 3D model is right. Now Claude prepares it for the real world — right size, right format, solid geometry.

💬 Say this to Claude Code
Prepare my Luffy King for 3D printing. Make it about 9 cm tall. Export as STL and repair the mesh.
Claude handles the full pipeline — resize, export, repair, quality check. All automatic.
  1. Remesh — optimizes the model and sets the height. Standard King is ~9 cm. Want bigger? Tell Claude.
  2. Export to STL — the format your 3D printer reads.
  3. Mesh repair — fixes surfaces, fills holes, removes floating fragments.
  4. Quality check — makes sure the model is solid, not hollow. If hollow, Claude regenerates.
  5. Final preview — what you see is exactly what will come out of the printer.
🏆
Mission complete. Open the STL in QIDIStudio, slice it, and print. That's YOUR Luffy King — designed by you, built from your concept art, repaired and sized for the printer. The first piece of the One Piece chess set.
