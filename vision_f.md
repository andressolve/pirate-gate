# Pirate Gate: One Piece

## The Game

You pick a pirate captain. You get their ship, their crew, their powers — everything. Then you and three friends sail the Grand Line against three other crews, all hunting for the same thing: the One Piece.

The ocean is open. Islands are scattered across the map. Marines patrol the shores. Devil Fruits are hidden in the water. And somewhere out there, the One Piece is waiting. Find it, grab it, get back to your ship, and escape — while every other crew is trying to do the same thing. Or trying to sink your ship before you can.

Your ship is your life. If it goes down, your whole crew is eliminated. Die on an island? You respawn on your ship. But if nobody's defending that ship while your crew is ashore, a rival captain might sail up and blow it apart. Every moment is a decision: who explores, who fights, who stays behind.

Matches last 30 minutes. Four crews. Sixteen players. One winner.

## The World

The Grand Line — the most dangerous ocean in the world. Open water stretches between scattered islands, each with its own terrain, its own dangers, its own secrets. Marines guard key positions. Sea currents push you off course. The weather is unpredictable.

The visual style is anime cel-shaded — bold outlines, vibrant colors, expressive character animation. It looks like the One Piece anime brought into a 3D game world. Third-person camera so you see every stretch of Luffy's arm, every swing of Zoro's swords, every snap of Arlong's jaws.

## The Factions

You don't build a crew. You pick a captain and inherit everything that captain has in the One Piece story — their ship, their crew, their Devil Fruit powers, their fighting style.

### Luffy — Straw Hat Pirates
- **Ship:** Going Merry
- **Crew:** Zoro, Nami, Usopp, Sanji
- **Identity:** The most versatile crew. Luffy has the Gum Gum Fruit — stretching punches with knockback, Gum Gum Gatling for rapid hits, Gum Gum Bazooka for big damage. Zoro is a close-range sword fighter with heavy damage. Sanji is fast kicks and mobility. Usopp fights at range with his slingshot. Nami uses her Clima-Tact for weather-based attacks.
- **Devil Fruit user:** Luffy (Gum Gum Fruit). The rest can pick up Devil Fruits during the match.
- **Ship ability:** Coup de Burst — a massive cannon blast that launches the Going Merry forward at high speed. Use it to escape, to ram, or to close distance fast.

### Buggy — Buggy Pirates
- **Ship:** Big Top
- **Crew:** Mohji, Cabaji, Richie, Alvida
- **Identity:** Tricky and unpredictable. Buggy has the Chop Chop Fruit — he splits apart to dodge attacks and sends body parts flying as weapons. Alvida has the Slip Slip Fruit — attacks slide off her. Mohji fights with Richie the lion. Cabaji is an acrobat swordsman. Two Devil Fruit users from the start — more than any other East Blue crew.
- **Devil Fruit users:** Buggy (Chop Chop), Alvida (Slip Slip)
- **Ship ability:** Buggy Ball — a massive cannonball that deals heavy damage to ships and structures. Raw destructive power.

### Don Krieg — Krieg Pirates
- **Ship:** Dreadnaught Sabre
- **Crew:** Gin, Pearl, Ideaman
- **Identity:** The war machine. No Devil Fruits — pure weaponry. Krieg wears heavy battle armor with hidden weapons: guns, bombs, a spear tipped with poison. Gin fights with iron tonfa. Pearl uses fire shields. This crew wins through firepower and armor, not special powers. Every crew member can pick up Devil Fruits during the match since none of them have eaten one.
- **Devil Fruit users:** None
- **Ship ability:** Iron Wall — the Dreadnaught Sabre deploys armored plating that temporarily makes it nearly invulnerable to cannon fire. Pure defense.

### Arlong — Arlong Pirates
- **Ship:** Shark Superb
- **Crew:** Hatchan, Chew, Kuroobi
- **Identity:** The ocean predators. Every member is a Fishman — naturally stronger than humans, and deadly in water. While Luffy and Buggy sink like rocks, Arlong's crew swims freely. They can dive underwater to approach ships unseen, fight in the ocean, and board from below. Arlong has monstrous bite strength and uses his own teeth as weapons. Hatchan wields six swords. Chew spits water bullets. Kuroobi is a Fishman Karate expert.
- **Devil Fruit users:** None — and strategically, they might NOT want to eat Devil Fruits they find, since it would cost them their water advantage.
- **Ship ability:** Dive — the Shark Superb submerges briefly, becoming invisible and untargetable. Resurface behind an enemy ship for a devastating ambush.

## Combat

Third-person, anime-style action. Every character plays differently.

**Basic attacks** are unique to each fighter — Zoro's sword slashes feel nothing like Sanji's rapid kicks or Usopp's slingshot shots. Each character has a basic attack combo and 2-3 special abilities mapped to buttons. Devil Fruit powers are ability buttons with cooldowns.

**When Luffy lands a Gum Gum Pistol**, his arm stretches back, snaps forward, connects — and the enemy flies back a short distance. You see the impact. You feel the hit. Every character's attacks have that weight and feedback.

**Controller support is built in.** This game is meant to be played with a controller in your hands.

**No Haki in version one.** The power systems are:
1. Devil Fruit abilities (unique to characters who have them)
2. Character-specific fighting styles (swords, kicks, weapons, Fishman Karate)
3. Fishman racial traits (water combat, enhanced strength)

## The Match Flow

**Pick phase.** Each of the four crews picks a captain. You get your ship, your crew roster. Each player on the crew picks which crew member to play.

**Sail phase.** You launch into the Grand Line. The ocean is open. Islands are visible in the distance. The One Piece location is unknown — you have to explore to find it.

**Explore and fight.** Sail to islands. Land with some or all of your crew. Fight Marines (AI) guarding the shores. Search for Devil Fruits in the water. Search for the One Piece. Encounter rival crews — fight on islands, on ships, on the open water.

**The choice.** Every moment: who goes ashore? Who defends the ship? All four of you exploring means faster searching but a vulnerable ship. Leave one behind and you're safer but slower. There is no right answer — it depends on what's happening around you.

**Ship combat.** Ships have cannons. Your ship has its unique ability. You can fire on enemy ships from range, or board them and fight the crew directly. Sink a ship and that entire crew is eliminated.

**The extraction.** Someone finds the One Piece. Get it back to your ship. Sail to the escape point. Every other crew now knows where you are — they're coming for you. Defend the ship. Protect the carrier. Escape the Grand Line.

**Win condition.** Escape with the One Piece, or be the last ship floating.

## The Coolest Moments

- Sailing toward an island and seeing another crew's ship already docked — do you land and fight, or circle around to the other side?
- Finding a Devil Fruit and deciding who eats it — give it to Zoro and he gets a new power, but now he can't swim
- Arlong's crew diving underwater and boarding your ship from below while you thought you were safe
- Your whole crew on an island and you hear cannons — someone's attacking your ship
- Grabbing the One Piece and the entire match pivoting to a desperate escape while three crews hunt you
- Using the Going Merry's Coup de Burst to launch past a blockade of two enemy ships

## What Makes This Game Special

This isn't just a One Piece skin on an existing game. The crew faction system — where picking a captain gives you a complete roster with asymmetric abilities — creates a strategy layer that Wildgate doesn't have. The Fishman water advantage, the Devil Fruit risk-reward, the ship-as-life mechanic, the who-stays-who-goes tension — these all come from One Piece's world and they all create real gameplay decisions.

Every match tells a different story. That's what One Piece is about.

## The First Version

Four captains. Four ships. The Grand Line with islands. Marines on shore. Devil Fruits in the water. Ship combat with cannons and unique ship abilities. Ground combat with character-specific fighting styles. The One Piece as the extraction objective.

That's the game. Everything else — more captains, Haki, Grand Line weather systems, Sea Kings — that's expansion. This version is complete on its own.

## The Future

Version two adds Grand Line saga captains: Crocodile, Enel, Doflamingo, Katakuri. Haki enters as a new mechanic. New islands, new ship types, new Marine bosses like the Admirals. The game grows the same way One Piece grows — each saga expands the world.
